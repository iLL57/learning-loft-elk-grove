How We Began

Our Story

The Learning Loft of Elk Grove was born from a conversation that many homeschool parents have had: How do we give our kids the richness of community, the joy of peer learning, and the breadth of hands-on experiences — without sacrificing what makes home education so special?

"We didn't want to recreate school. We wanted to create something better."

What began as a small cooperative among a few Elk Grove families grew into something more intentional — a place where children could arrive twice a week, roll up their sleeves, and discover something new about the world and themselves.

Today, The Learning Loft is a gathering place: for children who are learning to love learning, and for families who are building something meaningful together. We are deeply rooted in Elk Grove and in the belief that education is most powerful when it's personal, relational, and joyful.

What Guides Us

Our Core Values

These six values are not just words on a wall — they shape every rotation, every conversation, and every decision we make.

1

Curiosity First

We believe every child arrives with an innate desire to explore and understand. We protect that curiosity by letting questions lead the learning — never suppressing wonder in favor of rote answers.

2

Hands-On, Heart-In Learning

Children learn best by doing. We prioritize projects, experiments, and real experiences over worksheets. If a child can touch it, build it, grow it, or cook it — that's where we start.

3

Community & Belonging

Learning is richer in community. We cultivate a culture of kindness and inclusion where every child belongs unconditionally and every family feels like a partner, not a client.

4

Reverence for Nature

We believe time in the natural world is not optional — it's essential. Our garden, outdoor spaces, and nature-based curriculum foster a deep, lasting connection to the living world.

5

Family Partnership

Parents are the primary educators of their children, and we are honored to be their partners. We stay in open communication, welcome input, and never work against the family's vision.

6

Character Over Compliance

We care more about who a child is becoming than how perfectly they follow directions. Honesty, courage, empathy, and perseverance are woven into everything we do.
