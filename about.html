About The Learning Loft

A safe, welcoming space where children learn, build meaningful relationships, and thrive.

How We Began

Our Story

The Learning Loft was born from a deeply personal journey. When our family began homeschooling, we discovered both the freedom it offered and the challenges that came with it — we longed for a place where our children could build genuine friendships and learn alongside peers who truly got it.

"We weren't simply looking for a program—we were searching for a community."

We looked for programs that balanced real academics with creativity and hands-on learning, and found the options limited. So we built one, right here in Elk Grove — a place where kids roll up their sleeves, dig into projects, and build the kind of friendships that turn a homeschool day into something they look forward to.

Today, The Learning Loft is more than an enrichment program — we're a community where children are known and curiosity leads learning.

What We Believe

"Every child is a natural learner.
Our job is to protect that spark."

We believe the best education happens not despite home education, but because of it — and that a rich community of peers, mentors, and hands-on experiences makes it even more powerful.

What Guides Us

Our Core Values

These six values are not just words on a wall — they shape every rotation, every conversation, and every decision we make.

1

Curiosity First

We believe every child arrives with an innate desire to explore and understand. We protect that curiosity by letting questions lead the learning — never suppressing wonder in favor of rote answers.

2

Hands-On, Heart-In Learning

Children learn best by doing. We prioritize projects, experiments, and real experiences over worksheets. If a child can touch it, build it, grow it, or cook it — that's where we start.

3

Community & Belonging

Learning is richer in community. We cultivate a culture of kindness and inclusion where every child belongs unconditionally and every family feels like a partner, not a client.

4

Reverence for Nature

We believe time in the natural world is not optional — it's essential. Our garden, outdoor spaces, and nature-based curriculum foster a deep, lasting connection to the living world.

5

Family Partnership

Parents are the primary educators of their children, and we are honored to be their partners. We stay in open communication, welcome input, and never work against the family's vision.

6

Character Over Compliance

We care more about who a child is becoming than how perfectly they follow directions. Honesty, courage, empathy, and perseverance are woven into everything we do.
