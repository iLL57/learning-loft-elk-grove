What We Do

3 Enrichment Classrooms · 3 Rotations Daily

Our schedule is intentionally designed to maximize classroom use and minimize downtime. Every student experiences all three enrichment rooms each day — no one misses a thing.

Room A

Art Fusion (Tuesdays)

Visual arts, mixed media, maker projects, and creative expression through multiple artistic disciplines.

Foreign Language (Thursdays)

Conversational language learning in an immersive, playful environment.

Room B

Nature Science

Hands-on exploration of the natural world — plants, animals, outdoor observation, and environmental stewardship.

Room C

Life Skills

Real-world competence through cooking, sewing, practical problem-solving, and everyday independence.

10:20 – 11:20 AM

Core Academics (Homeroom)

Every session begins with a dedicated homeroom block where students build consistent academic relationships with a homeroom guide and their grade-level peers. This is where the foundational academics live.

Students are grouped by grade (1st & 2nd, 3rd & 4th, 5th & 6th) so instruction can be well-matched to their developmental stage.

Subjects Covered

  • ✦ English Language Arts — reading, writing, grammar
  • ✦ Social Studies — community, history, civics
  • ✦ Social Emotional Learning (SEL)
  • ✦ Small-group instruction and peer learning
  • ✦ Consistent homeroom relationships & academic support
ELA Social Studies SEL Small Group Grades 1–6
Room A

Art Fusion & Foreign Language

Tuesdays — Art Fusion: Creativity is not a talent — it's a practice. Children explore visual arts, mixed media, and maker projects in an environment where experimentation is celebrated and every child finds their creative voice.

Thursdays — Foreign Language: Conversational language learning in an immersive, playful setting designed to build genuine communication skills and cultural appreciation.

What Children Experience

  • ✦ Watercolor, acrylic & mixed media painting
  • ✦ Clay sculpting, collage & fiber arts
  • ✦ Maker projects and inventive building
  • ✦ Conversational foreign language (Thursdays)
  • ✦ Songs, games & cultural exploration
Visual Arts Mixed Media Maker Projects Foreign Language Cultural Studies
Room B

Nature Science

There is no better classroom than the living world. In our garden and outdoor spaces, children learn through direct engagement with nature — observing, hypothesizing, and discovering the science that surrounds them every day.

Nature journaling, outdoor exploration, and seasonal projects connect children to the rhythms of the earth and cultivate a deep sense of stewardship and wonder.

What Children Experience

  • ✦ Hands-on planting, tending & harvesting
  • ✦ Nature journaling and field sketching
  • ✦ Bug hunts, bird watching & outdoor observation
  • ✦ Composting, soil science & plant biology
  • ✦ Seasonal nature crafts and outdoor experiments
Nature Science Gardening Nature Journaling Outdoor Exploration Stewardship
Room C

Life Skills

One of the great gifts of home education is the freedom to teach what truly matters for life. Our Life Skills room gives children real-world competence, practical confidence, and the deep satisfaction of knowing how to do things.

From the kitchen to the sewing table, from tool use to financial literacy, children learn skills that will serve them for a lifetime.

What Children Experience

  • ✦ Cooking and kitchen skills (knife safety, recipes)
  • ✦ Beginner sewing and mending
  • ✦ Basic woodworking and tool use
  • ✦ Financial literacy — earning, saving & spending
  • ✦ First aid basics, community care & problem-solving
Cooking Sewing Woodworking Financial Literacy First Aid
A Typical Day

What to Expect When You Arrive

Every day at The Learning Loft is grounded in warmth, routine, and the freedom to be a kid who loves learning.

Warm Arrivals

Children are greeted by name each morning. Our welcome circle sets the tone for a day of intention, joy, and community.

Purposeful Rotations

Each block is thoughtfully planned with themes and projects that build across the semester. No two days look exactly alike.

Community Lunch

Shared meals matter. Lunch is an unhurried time for friendship, laughter, and the kind of connection that turns classmates into lifelong friends.
