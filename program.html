What We Do

Four Enrichment Rotations

Each rotation is guided by an experienced, caring adult who loves their subject and the children they teach. Activities rotate across the semester to provide variety and depth.

Rotation 1

Reading & Writing

Stories are at the heart of how humans make sense of the world. In this rotation, children explore the joy of both receiving and creating stories — through read-alouds, book discussions, journaling, and their own creative writing projects.

We meet children where they are as readers and writers, honoring every level while gently stretching each child's capacity and love for language.

What Children Experience

  • ✦ Read-alouds and guided literature discussions
  • ✦ Personal journaling and reflective writing
  • ✦ Creative fiction, poetry, and storytelling
  • ✦ Collaborative writing projects and sharing circles
  • ✦ Author studies and book club conversations
Literacy Creative Writing Storytelling Book Club Poetry
Rotation 2

Gardening & Nature

There is no better classroom than the living world. In our garden and outdoor spaces, children learn the full cycle of life — planting seeds, tending plants, observing insects, and harvesting food they've grown with their own hands.

Nature journaling, outdoor science exploration, and seasonal projects connect children to the rhythms of the earth and cultivate a deep sense of stewardship and wonder.

What Children Experience

  • ✦ Hands-on planting, tending & harvesting
  • ✦ Nature journaling and field sketching
  • ✦ Bug hunts, bird watching & outdoor observation
  • ✦ Composting and soil science
  • ✦ Seasonal nature crafts and celebrations
Gardening Nature Journaling Outdoor Science Botany Stewardship
Rotation 3

Art & Creativity

Creativity is not a talent — it's a practice. Our art rotation invites every child to explore visual arts, mixed media, and maker projects in an environment where experimentation is celebrated and there are no wrong answers.

From watercolor painting to clay sculpture, collage to fiber arts, children discover their own creative voice while building fine motor skills, spatial reasoning, and the courage to take creative risks.

What Children Experience

  • ✦ Watercolor, acrylic & mixed media painting
  • ✦ Clay sculpting and 3D projects
  • ✦ Collage, fiber arts & weaving
  • ✦ Maker projects and inventive building
  • ✦ Artist studies and art history connections
Visual Arts Sculpture Mixed Media Maker Projects Art History
Rotation 4

Life Skills

One of the great gifts of home education is the freedom to teach what truly matters for life. Our life skills rotation is built on that premise — giving children real-world competence, practical confidence, and the satisfaction of knowing how to do things.

From the kitchen to the garden shed, from the sewing table to the basics of managing money, children learn skills that will serve them for a lifetime.

What Children Experience

  • ✦ Cooking and kitchen skills (knife safety, recipes)
  • ✦ Beginner sewing and mending
  • ✦ Basic woodworking and tool use
  • ✦ Financial literacy — earning, saving & spending
  • ✦ First aid basics, community care & problem-solving
Cooking Sewing Woodworking Financial Literacy First Aid
A Typical Day

What to Expect When You Arrive

Every day at The Learning Loft is grounded in warmth, routine, and the freedom to be a kid who loves learning.

Warm Arrivals

Children are greeted by name each morning. Our welcome circle sets the tone for a day of intention, joy, and community.

Purposeful Rotations

Each block is thoughtfully planned with themes and projects that build across the semester. No two days look exactly alike.

Community Lunch

Shared meals matter. Lunch is an unhurried time for friendship, laughter, and the kind of connection that turns classmates into lifelong friends.
